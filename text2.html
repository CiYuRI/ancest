The PDP-10: Alan Kotok

Alan Kotok was the lead architect and designer of the PDP-10. Born on November 9, 1941, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, he attended the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and was one of the most recognizable human ambassadors of computers.

Kotok is considered one of the fathers of video games, computational games that humans enjoyed in leisure, playing a part in the creation of Spacewar! by giving Steve (Slug) Russell the sine-cosine routine needed to complete the game.

Kotok was also one of the founders of the World Wide Web Consortium, the organization that created standards for the internet, which further shifted humanity towards digitalization.
