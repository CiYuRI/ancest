The PDP-10: Technical Details

The PDP-10 comprised a series of 36-bit word systems, each with CPUs, memory, and other components, all linked by busses carried in point-point cables.

For storage and data transfer, high-speed mass storage device controllers were utilized, with the CPU controlling channels via the device controller.

Some examples of mass storage controllers include the RP10 disk controller with RP01, RP02, and RP03 disk drives, the RC10 disk/drum system with an RD10 fixed-head disk and RM10B drum, and the TM10 Magnetic Tape Control.
